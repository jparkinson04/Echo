# ECHO_BRIEF.md — Product context for Claude Code

This file gives Claude Code the full product context for Echo. Read this alongside CLAUDE.md at the start of every session.

---

## The problem

There is a serious and growing teacher retention crisis in the UK. Teachers are leaving the profession due to feeling unheard, unsupported, undervalued, and burned out. Schools and Multi-Academy Trusts have no structured, ongoing way to listen to their staff at scale. Exit interviews happen too late. Annual reviews are too infrequent.

Echo solves this by giving MATs and schools a monthly pulse on staff wellbeing, turning that data into actionable AI recommendations, and helping schools communicate their culture improvements — so they retain the teachers they have and attract new ones.

---

## What Echo does

Three core jobs:

1. **Listen** — monthly anonymous staff wellbeing surveys
2. **Understand** — AI-powered insights and recommendations for leaders
3. **Share** — beautiful newsletter and shareable social graphics from the data

---

## Who uses it

### MAT leaders (the buyer)
The Multi-Academy Trust buys Echo and rolls it out across their schools. MAT leaders want a high-level view across all schools — a heat map they can report to the board. They are not in the day-to-day.

### School leaders / headteachers (the operator)
The most frequent user. They log in to see their school's data, read AI recommendations, manage the newsletter, review staff submissions, and track ECTs.

### Teaching staff (the contributor)
Fill out the monthly survey via a link. No login required. Also submit shout-outs and blog posts. The experience must feel safe, quick, and worthwhile — not like another admin burden.

### ECTs — Early Career Teachers (protected group)
Teachers in their first two years. Highest risk of leaving. Tracked separately with their own check-in flow.

## Two login types

### MAT login
- Sees all schools in trust on one dashboard
- RAG heat map (Red/Amber/Green) per school per survey category
- Can drill into any individual school (read-only)
- Trust-wide trend charts
- Board report export (PDF)
- ECT risk overview across all schools

### School login
- Their school's dashboard only
- Full access: surveys, results, AI recommendations, newsletter, blog, ECT tracker
- Manages school accent colour and logo
- Invites staff via survey links

## Core features

### 1. Monthly staff wellbeing survey
- Built by the school using Echo's drag-and-drop survey builder
- Question types: 1–10 scale, multiple choice, free text, photo upload
- Categories: workload, feeling supported, leadership, belonging, professional development, work-life balance
- Anonymous by design — no names attached to responses, ever
- Sent via shareable link — staff click, fill in, done. No login.
- One survey per month per school
- Optional shout-out field at the end of every survey

### 2. School leader dashboard
Six cards:
1. Overall wellbeing score — /10, delta vs last month, 6-month sparkline
2. Category breakdown — horizontal bars per category
3. Survey response rate — ring/donut chart
4. AI insight — `✦ New insight` in school accent colour, one finding + recommendation
5. ECT risk monitor — On Track / Watch / At Risk counts
6. Latest staff voice — most recent approved shout-out

### 3. MAT dashboard
- Grid of all schools, each with RAG status
- Click school → drill into school dashboard (read-only)
- Trust-level trends over time
- Board report export

### 4. AI recommendations
Generated by Claude after each survey closes:
- Plain-English summary of what the data shows
- 3–5 specific actionable recommendations
- Risk flag if any category drops significantly month-on-month
- Suggested newsletter content

### 5. Qualitative staff voice
Free-text space for:
- Shout-outs for a colleague
- Something noticed that made the school feel good
- A suggestion for improvement
- A moment worth celebrating

Leaders review and approve before anything appears publicly. Approved submissions feed into the newsletter and recognition card.

### 6. Newsletter builder
- Leader clicks "Generate newsletter" — Claude produces structured draft
- Sections: welcome message, wellbeing snapshot, staff spotlight, from-the-team, looking ahead
- Drag to reorder sections
- Click any section to edit
- Drag and drop photos into sections
- School accent colour applied to header, dividers, CTA buttons automatically
- Export as HTML or copy to clipboard

### 7. Shareable social graphics (the "Strava moment")
After each survey, Echo generates a downloadable PNG graphic:
- Bold, clean design using school accent colour
- Key stat front and centre: e.g. "Staff wellbeing: 8.2/10 ↑"
- Response rate, top category, school name + logo
- Echo mark small in corner
- One-click download as PNG
- Schools post to LinkedIn, Twitter, their website — looks like their brand

### 8. ECT tracker
- ECTs receive a monthly (or fortnightly) check-in link — tokenised, no login
- Questions: settling in, mentor support, confidence, overall mood
- School leader dashboard: table of all ECTs with mood score, trend arrow, last check-in, risk status
- Risk calculated from: score average, trend direction, check-in frequency
- Click ECT → full profile: check-in history chart, notes timeline
- MAT leaders see ECT risk across all schools

### 9. Blog and staff voice feed
- Staff submit posts via link (no login needed)
- Types: shout-out, blog post, suggestion
- Leaders approve/reject from a review queue
- Approved posts appear in a staff feed and can be pulled into newsletter

### 10. Settings
- Organisation profile: name, logo upload
- Accent colour picker: colour wheel + hex input + 8 preset swatches
- Live preview panel updating in real time as colour changes
- Team management: invite users, assign roles, deactivate
- Survey schedule: set send day
- Future: email provider integration

## Accent colour system

Every school sets one custom accent colour that flows through everything they produce.

**Where it appears:**
- Newsletter header, CTA buttons, section dividers
- Shareable social graphic
- Recognition spotlight cards
- AI insight badges
- Onboarding/celebration moments

**Where it does NOT appear:**
- Navigation (that uses Electric Blue #1B6EF3)
- Data charts and bars (Electric Blue)
- Error/status indicators (semantic colours only)

**How it works:**
```ts
// On login, after fetching org data:
injectAccentColour(organisation.accent_colour ?? '#F72585');

// Injects:
// --color-pop      → the hex
// --color-pop-light → lightened 20%
// --color-pop-bg   → hex at 12% opacity
```

Default if not set: `#F72585` (Hot Pink)

**Preset swatches:**

## Database schema

```sql
create table organisations (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  name text not null,
  type text check (type in ('school','mat')) default 'school',
  mat_id uuid references organisations(id),
  accent_colour text default '#F72585',
  logo_url text,
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

create table users (
  id uuid primary key references auth.users,
  organisation_id uuid references organisations(id),
  role text check (role in ('mat_admin','school_admin','leader','staff','ect')) default 'staff',
  full_name text,
  email text,
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

create table surveys (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  organisation_id uuid references organisations(id),
  title text not null,
  description text,
  questions jsonb not null default '[]',
  status text check (status in ('draft','active','closed')) default 'draft',
  type text check (type in ('staff','ect')) default 'staff',
  created_by uuid references users(id),
  created_at timestamptz default now(),
  closes_at timestamptz
);

create table survey_responses (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  survey_id uuid references surveys(id),
  organisation_id uuid references organisations(id),
  answers jsonb not null default '{}',
  submitted_at timestamptz default now()
  -- no user_id — anonymous by design
);

create table ect_checkins (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  organisation_id uuid references organisations(id),
  ect_user_id uuid references users(id),
  mood_score int check (mood_score between 1 and 10),
  support_score int check (support_score between 1 and 10),
  confidence_score int check (confidence_score between 1 and 10),
  notes text,
  submitted_at timestamptz default now()
);

create table staff_submissions (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  organisation_id uuid references organisations(id),
  author_id uuid references users(id),
  type text check (type in ('shoutout','blog','suggestion')) default 'shoutout',
  nominee_name text,
  title text,
  content text,
  anonymous boolean default false,
  status text check (status in ('pending','approved','rejected')) default 'pending',
  submitted_at timestamptz default now()
);

create table newsletters (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  organisation_id uuid references organisations(id),
  title text,
  content_json jsonb,
  content_html text,
  generated_at timestamptz default now(),
  sent_at timestamptz,
  status text check (status in ('draft','sent')) default 'draft'
);

create table shareable_graphics (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  organisation_id uuid references organisations(id),
  survey_id uuid references surveys(id),
  graphic_url text,
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

create table ect_tokens (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  ect_user_id uuid references users(id),
  organisation_id uuid references organisations(id),
  token text unique not null default gen_random_uuid()::text,
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);
```

## API routes

### POST /api/generate-newsletter
Input:
```ts
{
  organisation_id: string;
  survey_id: string;
}
```
Fetches survey results, staff submissions, and org data from Supabase. Calls Claude. Returns newsletter JSON.

### POST /api/generate-insight
Input:
```ts
{
  organisation_id: string;
  survey_id: string;
}
```
Called automatically when a survey closes. Returns `{ insight: string, recommendations: string[] }`.

### POST /api/send-survey
Input:
```ts
{
  survey_id: string;
  emails: string[];
}
```
Sends survey link emails via Resend. Each email contains the unique link `/s/[survey_id]`.

### POST /api/send-checkin
Input:
```ts
{
  ect_user_id: string;
}
```
Looks up ECT token, sends check-in link to ECT's email via Resend.

## Newsletter AI prompt

```ts
const prompt = `You are generating a warm, professional monthly staff newsletter for ${orgName}, a UK primary school.

Tone: warm, human, celebratory where possible. Never corporate. Never clinical.

Data:
- Overall wellbeing score: ${score}/10 (${delta > 0 ? '+' : ''}${delta} vs last month)
- Category scores: ${JSON.stringify(categoryScores)}
- Response rate: ${responseRate}% (${responded} of ${total} staff)
- Key insight: ${topInsight}
- Staff submissions: ${JSON.stringify(staffSubmissions)}

Generate exactly these sections:
1. welcome — warm opening from leadership, 2-3 sentences
2. wellbeing_snapshot — brief, data-driven, positive framing
3. staff_spotlight — feature the top shout-out
4. from_the_team — summarise approved blog posts or suggestions
5. looking_ahead — forward-looking close, 1-2 sentences

Return ONLY valid JSON, no markdown:
{
  "title": "string",
  "sections": [
    { "type": "welcome", "heading": "string", "content": "string" },
    { "type": "wellbeing_snapshot", "heading": "string", "content": "string" },
    { "type": "staff_spotlight", "heading": "string", "content": "string" },
    { "type": "from_the_team", "heading": "string", "content": "string" },
    { "type": "looking_ahead", "heading": "string", "content": "string" }
  ]
}`;
```

## User journeys (build these flows, in this order)

### Priority 1 — core loop
1. Leader logs in → dashboard loads with their school's data
2. Leader creates a survey → sends link to staff
3. Staff fills in survey (no login) → response recorded anonymously
4. Survey closes → AI generates insight → appears on dashboard

### Priority 2 — communication
5. Leader generates newsletter from survey data
6. Leader edits and exports newsletter as HTML
7. Leader downloads shareable social graphic

### Priority 3 — ECT
8. Leader views ECT tracker → sees risk statuses
9. Leader sends check-in link to ECT
10. ECT fills in check-in (no login) → risk recalculated

### Priority 4 — MAT view
11. MAT admin logs in → sees trust heat map
12. MAT admin drills into a school → read-only dashboard
13. MAT admin exports board report

---

## What Echo is NOT

- Not a counselling or mental health tool
- Not an HR complaints system
- Not a performance management tool for individual teachers
- Not a data dump — everything must be readable, beautiful, and actionable
